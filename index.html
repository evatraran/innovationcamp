Digitalizing attendence in India

The Problem

Statistics from Unicef shows that 29% of pupils in India do not complete primary school, and often it is those with lower income that does not manage to follow through. Education is valuable but often other factors can get in the way such as hunger, bad environment, or that the child needs to stay at home to look after their family.

In 2015 88,3% was registered in school, most of these in public school. However, many of these dropped out and in Junior High School the attendance is only 44%. It is obligatory to take attendance in India, but it isnâ€™t always followed through and what the real attendance is and the causes of the low attendance is uncertain. To secure that children in India can keep their school attendance we want to make a systematic solution that map out the reasons for absence.

The Solution

Our solution is making an app where teachers can digitalize the attendence of their students every day. The teachers takes the attendence on paper as usual. They can then scan the paper with a smart phone or scanner. This data is submitted into the app which sorts the data and creates a database.

This database can be used to identify trends in attendance and the reasons behind it. In the long run, hopefully the app can also help the school identify students that needs help to stay in school.
